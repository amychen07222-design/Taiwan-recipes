台灣好滋味

Three beloved Taiwanese recipes to bring a little bit of Taiwan into your kitchen.

珍珠奶茶
Bubble Milk Tea
10 min
Prep
20 min
Cook
2
Servings
Ingredients
Dry tapioca pearls½ cup
Black tea bags2
Whole milk1 cup
Brown sugar3 tbsp
Ice cubes1 cup
Water4 cups
Steps
1
Cook boba. Boil 4 cups water. Add tapioca pearls and cook 15–20 min stirring occasionally. Cover and rest 15 min.
2
Sweeten. Drain boba and toss with 2 tbsp brown sugar. Set aside.
3
Brew tea. Steep 2 bags in 1 cup hot water for 5 min. Stir in 1 tbsp sugar. Let cool.
4
Assemble. Add boba to a glass. Fill with ice, pour cooled tea and milk. Stir with a wide straw and enjoy!
Tip: Find tapioca pearls at any Asian grocery store. They come in different sizes — medium is easiest to work with at home.
Recipe adapted from hungryhuy.com
台灣涼麵
Taiwanese Cold Noodles
10 min
Prep
10 min
Cook
2
Servings
Ingredients
Wheat or egg noodles200g
Sesame oil1 tbsp
Cucumber, julienned½
Peanut butter3 tbsp
Soy sauce2 tbsp
Black vinegar1 tbsp
Sugar1 tsp
Garlic, minced2 cloves
Warm water2–3 tbsp
Steps
1
Cook noodles al dente per package. Drain and rinse under cold water until cool.
2
Toss with sesame oil so they don't stick together. Refrigerate while making sauce.
3
Make sauce. Whisk peanut butter, soy sauce, black vinegar, sugar, and garlic. Add warm water 1 tbsp at a time until pourable.
4
Serve. Top noodles with cucumber and drizzle sauce generously. Toss and enjoy cold!
Tip: Add chili oil for a kick! Leftovers keep well in the fridge — just add a splash of water to loosen the sauce.
Recipe adapted from chejorge.com
滷肉飯
Braised Pork Rice · Lu Rou Fan
15 min
Prep
1.5 hr
Cook
4
Servings
Ingredients
Pork belly, skin-on, cubed600g
Steamed white rice4 bowls
Hard boiled eggs4
Fried shallots3 tbsp
Garlic, minced4 cloves
Soy sauce4 tbsp
Dark soy sauce2 tbsp
Rice wine (Shaoxing)3 tbsp
Brown sugar2 tbsp
Five spice powder½ tsp
Water600 ml
Steps
1
Brown pork. Cook pork belly in a pot over medium-high heat until browned. Add garlic and cook 1 more minute.
2
Add sauce. Stir in both soy sauces, rice wine, brown sugar, and five spice powder until combined.
3
Braise. Pour in water and bring to a boil. Add fried shallots and eggs. Reduce to a gentle simmer, cover, and cook for 1–1.5 hours until pork is very tender.
4
Serve pork and sauce generously over steamed rice with a halved egg on top. Enjoy!
Tip: Tastes even better the next day as the flavours deepen! Freeze leftovers for up to 1 month.
Recipe adapted from tiffycooks.com
